Chapter 5: Interviews

Overview

Interviews require a unique combination of preparation and apparent spontaneity. Whether you are interviewing for a job, being interviewed by a journalist, hosting a podcast, or pitching to a potential client, you need to appear natural and confident while delivering precise, well-considered answers.

The challenge is that preparation and naturalness are in tension. Over-prepare and you sound scripted. Under-prepare and you miss key points, stumble on difficult questions, or fail to communicate your value clearly.

MGenie resolves this tension by giving you a prepared script that you read, not memorize. Your delivery stays natural because you are not searching for words. Your content stays accurate because you prepared it carefully.

Interview Types and Script Approaches

Different interview formats require different scripting strategies.

Job Interview

Challenge: Structured questions, need to demonstrate competency, competitive stakes.

Script approach: Prepare full responses to the top 20 most common questions for your role. Include specific examples using the STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result). Each answer should be 90-120 seconds when delivered.

Media Interview / Podcast

Challenge: Conversational format, unexpected follow-ups, need to stay on message.

Script approach: Prepare three to five key messages you want to communicate regardless of what is asked. Write brief talking points rather than full answers. Use AI Answer for unexpected directions.

Client / Sales Call

Challenge: Discovery questions, objections, need to tailor messaging in real time.

Script approach: Write a discovery script with prepared questions. Include objection-handling talking points. Use AI Answer when clients raise issues you have not specifically prepared for.

Technical Interview

Challenge: Problem-solving under pressure, whiteboard or coding questions, system design.

Script approach: Prepare introductory and closing scripts. Write talking points for behavioral questions. For technical questions, use AI Answer sparingly to recall specific details (library APIs, performance characteristics, etc.).

TTS Rehearsal for Interview Preparation

TTS rehearsal is exceptionally valuable for interview preparation because it forces you to hear your answers as someone else hears them.

What to listen for:

  • Timing: Is the answer too long (over 2 minutes) or too short (under 30 seconds)?
  • Clarity: Does the answer get to the point quickly, or does it bury the main message?
  • Specificity: Does the answer include concrete examples, or does it stay abstract?
  • Energy: Does the answer sound enthusiastic and confident, or flat and rehearsed?
  • Jargon: Does the answer use too much technical jargon for a non-technical interviewer?

Iteration process:

  1. Play TTS for an answer
  2. Note issues (too long, weak opening, missing example)
  3. Edit the script
  4. Play TTS again
  5. Repeat until the answer sounds the way you want to sound in the actual interview

After three or four TTS passes, the answer is usually well-polished. On the day of the interview, a final TTS run refreshes your familiarity with the content.

Using Stage Directions for Interview Pacing

Interviews are high-stress environments where pacing breaks down. You speak faster, you fill silence, you rush through key points. Stage directions in your script act as behavioral anchors.

Recommended interview stage directions:

Direction When to Use
[PAUSE 2s] After a complex answer, let the point land
[SLOW DOWN] When you notice yourself rushing
[BREATHE] Before a difficult question answer
[SMILE] Reminder to maintain warmth and engagement
[EYE CONTACT] Look up from the script at the interviewer
[CHECK TIME] Keep awareness of total interview duration
[TRANSITION] Moving from one part of an answer to another
[ASK QUESTION] Reminder to ask one of your prepared questions

The AI Answer Feature in Interviews

The AI Answer feature is a critical tool for live interviews when an unexpected question arises.

Protocol for using AI Answer in interviews:

  1. Interviewer asks an unexpected question
  2. Say: "That is a great question. Let me think about that for a moment."
  3. Quickly press the AI Answer button
  4. Read the suggested response (takes 2-4 seconds)
  5. Begin your answer, using the suggestion as a starting point

The 3-5 second pause is entirely natural in an interview context. Interviewers expect candidates to think before answering.

Context files make AI Answer more powerful: If you have loaded your resume, the job description, and relevant portfolio documents as context files, AI Answer draws on all of that information when generating suggestions. The responses are specific to your background and the role, not generic.

For technical interviews: Load relevant documentation (framework docs, API references, system design patterns) as context files. AI Answer can recall specific technical details that are hard to memorize.

Step-by-Step Setup for Interviews

Step 1 - Research and write the script (2-3 days before)

Research the company, role, and common questions. Write full answers to the 15-20 most likely questions. Include at least one specific example per answer. Write your 30-second introduction and your closing questions.

Step 2 - TTS rehearsal (1-2 days before)

Run TTS on every answer. Fix issues. Time each answer. A complete run of all prepared answers should take 20-30 minutes.

Step 3 - Load context files

In Settings > Context, add: - Your resume or CV (PDF or text) - The job description - Any relevant portfolio documents - Company overview or recent press release

These become available to AI Answer during the interview.

Step 4 - Final TTS run (morning of interview)

Do a 10-minute TTS run covering your introduction and your top five answers. This refreshes the content in your working memory without over-rehearsal.

Step 5 - Configure MGenie for interview mode

  • Set font size to 28-32pt (readable at a quick glance)
  • Enable semi-transparent background
  • Position the window at the top of the screen near your camera
  • Enable recording for self-review
  • Disable voice activation (you want manual scroll control in interviews)

Step 6 - During the interview

Follow your script for prepared questions. Use Back and Forward buttons to navigate. Use AI Answer for unexpected questions. Remember the stage direction cues.

Step 7 - Post-interview review

Play back the recording. Run AI Summary to generate coaching notes. Review which answers felt strong and which felt weak. Update the script for future interviews.

Pro Tips for Interviews

Write answers in your own voice

If the script sounds like a formal document, it will sound that way when you read it. Write the script using the words you would naturally say. If you would not say "leverage core competencies" in normal conversation, do not write it.

Prepare one great story per answer

The best interview answers are specific stories, not general descriptions of skills. For every question type ("Tell me about a time..."), have a prepared story with a clear situation, action, and result.

Use TTS to catch accidental jargon

When you hear your answer played back, jargon that seemed fine when writing it often sounds alienating. Simplify aggressively.

Record and review

A recording of a mock interview (or a real one) is the most useful feedback tool available. The recording shows you exactly how you come across, which is often very different from how you imagine you come across.
