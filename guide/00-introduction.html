MGenie User Guide

Introduction

Welcome to the MGenie User Guide. MGenie is a floating teleprompter for macOS that lives at the top of your screen, keeping your script visible during any call, recording, or presentation without obscuring your camera view or your audience.

This guide covers everything from first launch to building a long-term knowledge base from your meetings. Whether you use MGenie for daily standups, formal presentations, job interviews, or team brainstorms, each chapter walks through a specific use case with practical setup instructions and real-world examples.

What Is MGenie?

MGenie is a macOS menu bar application that displays a floating, semi-transparent teleprompter window anchored to the top of your screen. It is designed to be invisible to screen-sharing and recording software while remaining fully visible to you, so you can read your script naturally while appearing composed and prepared on camera.

Core Features at a Glance

Scrolling Script Display Shows your script in a readable, auto-scrolling window at the top of the screen

Status indicator (top-left): Green dot means the window is excluded from screen capture. The clock shows elapsed session time.

Script area (center): Your formatted script text, auto-scrolling as you speak. The current word is highlighted during TTS playback.

Progress bar (bottom-right of toolbar): Shows your position through the script.

Hover controls (bottom toolbar): Appear when you hover over the window. Grouped by function.

Floating Control Panel

The control panel is a compact, always-on-top toolbar that floats independently from the prompter. You can drag it anywhere on screen.

+--------------------------------------------------------------+
| Play Back Fwd Reset | TTS | Rec New Exp | AI |
+--------------------------------------------------------------+
 Transport TTS Recording/Session AI/View

Transport: Play/pause scrolling, scroll back, scroll forward, reset to top

TTS: Toggle Text-to-Speech playback with word highlighting

Recording/Session: Start/stop recording, new session, export session

AI: Run AI Summary, run AI Answer, toggle visibility

Quick-Start Checklist

New to MGenie? Complete these five steps and you will be ready for your first session in under ten minutes.

Step 1 - Launch MGenie

Open MGenie from your Applications folder or Launchpad. The menu bar icon appears in the top-right of your menu bar. Click it to show the prompter and control panel.

Step 2 - Write your script

Click the menu bar icon, then open Settings > Script. Type or paste your script. Use plain text or Markdown formatting. Save when done.

Step 3 - Configure your voice

Go to Settings > Voice. Select your preferred TTS voice and speaking rate. Click the preview button to hear a sample. Adjust until the speed and tone feel natural for your use case.

Step 4 - Set up AI (optional but recommended)

Go to Settings > AI. Enter your OpenAI API key. Set your Knowledge Base folder. This is where exported summaries will be saved after each session.

Step 5 - Run a dry rehearsal

Press the TTS button in the control panel to start playback. Listen to your script read back to you. Adjust speed and font size as needed. When satisfied, press the Record button to begin a live session.

Technical Requirements

  • macOS 13 Ventura or later is required
  • Apple Silicon or Intel: Universal binary, runs natively on both architectures
  • Microphone access: Required for recording and voice activation
  • Internet connection: Required for AI Summary and AI Answer features
  • OpenAI API key: Required for AI features (obtain at platform.openai.com)

MGenie does not require any subscription. AI features consume OpenAI API credits at standard rates based on the number of tokens processed.

A Note on Privacy

MGenie processes audio locally for transcription using Apple on-device speech recognition. Transcripts are only sent to OpenAI when you explicitly trigger an AI Summary or AI Answer action. The prompter window is designed to be excluded from screen capture; the green status dot in the top-left corner confirms this is active. Your Knowledge Base files are stored locally and are never uploaded automatically.
