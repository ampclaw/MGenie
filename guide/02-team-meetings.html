Chapter 2: Team Meetings

Overview

Team meetings are the backbone of collaborative work: sprint reviews, project syncs, 1-on-1s, all-hands, retrospectives, and planning sessions. These meetings typically run 30 to 60 minutes, involve multiple speakers, and require a clear agenda to stay productive.

Without structure, team meetings drift. A 45-minute planning session becomes a 90-minute conversation with no clear outcomes. Attendees leave unclear on next steps, and follow-up emails reconstruct what was decided.

MGenie helps meeting facilitators maintain agenda control, capture everything that is said, and produce clean summaries and action items at the close of each session.

How MGenie Helps Team Meetings

Agenda as Script

Instead of a flowing narrative script, use your meeting agenda as the prompter content. Each agenda item becomes a section in your script with its allocated time and talking points. As the facilitator, you read from the agenda rather than improvising transitions between topics.

This approach produces more disciplined meetings. When the prompter shows you are on item 3 of 6 and 40 minutes have elapsed, you know to move on.

Session Recording Per Agenda Item

For longer meetings with distinct agenda items, use the New Session button between items. This creates a separate recording and transcript for each agenda item, which makes the AI Summary more focused and the Knowledge Base entries more organized.

AI Summary at Close

At the end of the meeting, press AI Summary. MGenie generates a structured summary of the transcript covering:

  • Key discussion points
  • Decisions made
  • Action items and owners
  • Open questions for follow-up

This summary is ready to paste into your team wiki, email, or Knowledge Base.

Knowledge Base for Meeting Minutes

Export each session to the Knowledge Base using the export button. Over time, this builds a searchable archive of meeting history that any team member can reference.

Setting Up a Multi-Item Agenda

The key to using MGenie for team meetings is formatting your agenda script for easy navigation.

Formatting Conventions

Use brackets for stage directions, section headers for agenda items, and bullet points for talking points:

[FACILITATOR: Sarah Chen | DATE: 2024-01-15 | SPRINT: 23]

Agenda Item 1: Sprint Review (10 min)

- Velocity: 34 story points completed vs 38 planned
- Highlights: Auth module shipped, mobile layout improved
- Demo: Tom will share screen for checkout flow demo

[PAUSE - ask Tom to share screen]

Agenda Item 2: Retrospective (15 min)

- What went well this sprint?
- What could be improved?
- Action items from last retro: check status

[OPEN TO TEAM - collect responses]

Agenda Item 3: Sprint Planning (20 min)

- Capacity review: who is out next sprint?
- Priority items from backlog
- Story point estimation for top 5 items

Agenda Item 4: Announcements (5 min)

- Engineering all-hands next Thursday
- New onboarding doc in Confluence
- Q3 OKR review scheduled for the 22nd

Using Stage Directions

Stage directions in square brackets give you real-time reminders during the meeting:

  • [PAUSE] - stop talking and wait for responses
  • [SHOW SLIDE] - switch to your slide deck
  • [OPEN TO QUESTIONS] - invite the team to speak
  • [CHECK TIME] - glance at the elapsed timer
  • [TRANSITION] - signal moving to the next agenda item
  • [ACTION ITEM] - flag something for the summary

Stage directions are visible only to you in the prompter. They do not appear in any recording or export.

Step-by-Step Setup Guide

Step 1 - Create the agenda script

At least 30 minutes before the meeting, open Settings > Script and write the agenda using the format above. Include time allocations for each item. Include stage directions at transition points.

Step 2 - Name the session

Give the meeting a descriptive name like "Sprint-23-Review-2024-01-15". This name appears in the Knowledge Base export filename, making it easy to find later.

Step 3 - Set recording preferences

In Settings > Behavior, confirm that recording is enabled and your microphone is selected. For best transcription accuracy, ensure your microphone is working and positioned well.

Step 4 - Open MGenie at meeting start

Launch the prompter window before joining the call. Confirm the status dot is green (excluded from screen capture). Reset the script position to the top.

Step 5 - Start recording

Press the Record button. The recording indicator appears in the prompter window. You are now capturing audio and generating a live transcript.

Step 6 - Facilitate using the prompter

Follow the script for each agenda item. Use the Back and Forward buttons to navigate if you need to revisit a section. Follow stage directions as you reach them.

Step 7 - Use New Session between major agenda items

After completing a major agenda item, press New Session. This starts a fresh transcript for the next item. The previous transcript is preserved and can be exported separately.

Step 8 - Run AI Summary at close

After the final agenda item, stop recording and press AI Summary. Review the generated summary. Edit if needed, then export to the Knowledge Base.

Step 9 - Share the summary

Copy the AI Summary output and paste it into your team communication channel, wiki, or email. The structured format (Key Points, Decisions, Action Items) is immediately usable as meeting minutes.

Example Meeting Script

A complete example for a 45-minute sprint retrospective:

[SPRINT 23 RETROSPECTIVE | 2024-01-15 | FACILITATOR: Maria]
[Attendees: Full engineering team]
[Target duration: 45 minutes]

Opening (2 min)

Good afternoon everyone. Thank you for joining the Sprint 23
retrospective. Today we have 45 minutes to reflect on what
went well, identify improvements, and set clear action items
for the next sprint.

[START RECORDING]

What Went Well (10 min)

Let us start with the positives. What went particularly well
in Sprint 23?

[OPEN TO TEAM - collect 4 to 6 responses]

I will summarize what I am hearing:
- The auth module shipped on schedule
- Code review turnaround improved significantly
- Daily standup quality has been excellent

Anything else before we move on?

[PAUSE]

What Could Be Improved (10 min)

Now the improvement areas. What frustrated you this sprint,
or what would you change going forward?

[OPEN TO TEAM - collect 4 to 6 items]

Themes I am hearing:
- Ticket estimation accuracy needs work
- Communication between frontend and backend
- Staging environment reliability

[PAUSE - vote on top two items if needed]

Action Items from Last Retro (5 min)

Before we set new action items, let us check Sprint 22 actions.

Action 1: Automated deployment pipeline - [STATUS?]
Action 2: Documentation update in onboarding repo - [STATUS?]
Action 3: Move standup to 9:30am - Done, confirmed.

[CAPTURE STATUS FROM TEAM]

New Action Items (10 min)

Based on today, let us set two or three action items with
clear owners and deadlines.

Action 1: Fix staging environment reliability
 Owner: DevOps team, due Sprint 24 day 3.

Action 2: Introduce frontend/backend sync every Tuesday 2pm
 Owner: Tech leads, starting next sprint.

Does everyone agree? Any modifications?

[PAUSE - confirm]

Closing (3 min)

Great retrospective today. Summary:

- Top positive: Auth module shipped, code review speed up
- Top improvement: Staging reliability and cross-team comms
- Two action items assigned with clear owners

Meeting notes will be in Slack within the hour.
Thank you all. See you at standup tomorrow.

[STOP RECORDING]
[RUN AI SUMMARY]
[EXPORT TO KB: sprint-23-retro]

Using the New Session Button

The New Session button creates a fresh recording context while preserving previous transcripts. Use it to:

  • Separate the sprint review transcript from the retrospective
  • Give each agenda item its own AI Summary
  • Export items individually to the Knowledge Base with descriptive names

This is especially valuable for long meetings with distinct topics. A two-hour planning session might produce three or four separate Knowledge Base entries, each focused on a specific topic.

AI Summary for Meeting Minutes

After running AI Summary, the output typically includes:

Key Points: The main topics discussed and their outcomes

Decisions Made: Specific decisions the team agreed upon

Action Items: Tasks assigned during the meeting with owners

Open Questions: Items needing follow-up or further investigation

You can improve AI Summary quality by adding context files in Settings > Context. Loading your team wiki or previous meeting notes lets the AI reference prior decisions when summarizing.

Pro Tips for Meeting Facilitators

Prepare the script the day before

A facilitator who reads from a prepared agenda signals respect for the team time. Members notice when facilitators are organized.

Use time markers in the script

Annotate each agenda item with its target end time. "Item 2 should end by :20 past the hour." This lets you check the elapsed timer and decide whether to move on.

Keep the prompter window narrow

A narrow prompter takes less screen real estate and is less likely to conflict with a shared screen or video tiles. In Settings > Layout, reduce the window width to 60-70 pct of the screen.

Record every meeting, not just important ones

The cost of recording is low. The benefit of having a searchable transcript of a meeting that produced unexpected decisions is high.

Let AI Answer handle unexpected questions

If a team member asks a question outside your prepared agenda, use the AI Answer button. It generates a response based on the transcript and any context files you have loaded.
